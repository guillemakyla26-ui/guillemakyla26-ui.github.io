Y2K Aesthetic
Y2K Aesthetic
A nostalgic, techno-optimistic style blending futuristic tech vibes with 2000s pop culture. Defined by shiny metallics, glossy white and icy blue tones, and “blobby” 3D graphics, it reflects the excitement of the early internet era.
Pop Art
Pop Art
A mid-1950s–1960s art movement originating in Britain and America that challenged fine art traditions by incorporating mass-culture imagery, such as advertisements, comic books, and consumer products. Defined by bold colors, irony, and mechanical techniques like screen printing, it transformed mundane objects into high art.
